[Nordhealth](https://www.nordhealth.com/) flew us all out to [Alicante](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alicante) for the company offsite. This was the big one - meeting everybody in person for the first time. I'd already met the marketing team in [Copenhagen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Copenhagen) about a month earlier, but this was the full roster. Engineers, designers, product people, the lot.

## Getting There

I met up with Nikolaj, Ricardo and Pedro at the airport before the flight down. The flight in was beautiful - coming down along the [Costa Blanca](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Costa_Blanca) coastline you get that classic Mediterranean postcard moment, deep blue water against dry, sun-bleached land. I knew immediately that sunscreen was going to be important. Spoiler: I did not act on this knowledge.

We grabbed a cab from the [airport](https://www.openstreetmap.org/way/83560647) to the hotel together, and from there it was just a steady stream of faces I'd only known through screens. I met my new manager Andrea, my good friend Naresh, Ryan, and I finally got to meet my teammate David in person. I met Fred and Arunas - the legend. And eventually my roommate for the duration, Giorgios.

## The Offsite

The days were packed. Work sessions, talks, and all the in-between conversations that make these trips worthwhile. The highlight on the talks side was a security lecture by Cezary - genuinely impressive stuff about software security. I'd say about 20-25% flew over my head, but that's what you'd expect from someone that well-versed in the field. The kind of talk where you walk away feeling both smarter and more aware of how much you don't know.

There was also a meditation class led by Siddhartha, who had flown in all the way from [India](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India). I joined in, and afterwards got to talk to him and ask him some questions, which I'm grateful for.

And then there was the [karaoke](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Karaoke). Most of it was what you'd expect, but one guy decided to go full [brutal death metal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brutal_death_metal). And he could *belt*. Genuinely hilarious and genuinely impressive at the same time.

Beyond the scheduled stuff it was just lots of drinks, walking around with newfound colleagues, and getting to know people. Happy to meet Philip, the designer. Joana, who aided in arranging the whole thing. And Karan, the CEO, of course. The food was great every night, but one dessert stood out - a bread pudding type deal topped with an ice cream that had hints of smoke, rich deep sugars, and [dulce de leche](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dulce_de_leche). One of those dishes where you stop talking mid-sentence because your brain needs a moment.

## Castillo De Santa Barbara

On the last day a group of us - the gang - took a walk up to [**Castillo De Santa Barbara**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Castle_of_Santa_B%C3%A1rbara). We took the [lift](https://www.openstreetmap.org/way/317870498) up to the castle entrance and hiked around from there. The views over [Alicante](https://www.openstreetmap.org/relation/11317099) and the coastline were stunning, and they had medieval armor on display inside which was a nice bonus.

Filip was shade-maxxing for most of it, which was the smart move. I, on the other hand, did the entire walk without sunscreen, like an authentic idiot. Frittata mode: engaged.
